Boston University College of Communication courses taught by Prof. Jen Jiun Choi

Undergraduate Courses: Visual Graphic Design
These courses introduce visual design fundamentals to students with little to no prior design experience

CM501 • Design Strategy & Software 1

Provides a comprehensive introduction to the design process, creative mindset, and design ethics. Students learn core static design principles such as typography, color theory, hierarchy, and layout while building essential creative software skills.

Projects: Visual Identity, Campaign Design

CM529 • Design Strategy & Software 2
Coming Spring 2026

Expands on CM501 by introducing motion and interactivity. Students learn foundational principles of moving images and apply static design concepts to dynamic environments, with an emphasis on interface design

Projects: Motion Design, Interactive Prototype

CM 501 Student Project Samples • Fall 25

Graduate Courses: Product Development & User Experience
Part of the Master of Science in Human-Centered Design program these courses teach the holistic product-development cycle to an interdisciplinary student body.

CM7XX • Human-Centered Design 1 (UX)
Coming Fall 2026

Guides students through the end-to-end solution development process using a strategic, human-centered approach. Students conduct user research, synthesize insights, frame problems, and generate and validate design concepts that prioritize empathy, inclusivity, and real-world impact.

Project: UX Research & Product Case Study

CM7XX • Product Management & Leadership
Coming Spring 2027

Prepares students to drive product vision, create lasting value, and influence organizational culture. The course covers core competencies such as feature prioritization, roadmap planning, Agile methodology, and stakeholder management. Through case studies and role-play, students learn to lead human-centered teams, measure impact, and align design vision with business objectives.

Project: Product Strategy & Roadmap
